Frequently Asked Questions

What is ESTEEM?

With a degree in ESTEEM students are immersed in solving problems to bring emerging technology to life with their thesis. Throughout the program, students work with a startup company to launch a new business or with an established company to bring a new product to market. In addition, the students are rigorously tested in academics, write a business plan, model financial forecasts, conduct market research and interview potential customers. ESTEEM students learn in the classroom, then practice the knowledge with their thesis project. For more information on the degree, please click here.

What can you do with a degree in ESTEEM?

A degree in ESTEEM gives students the ability to write highly detailed business plans, conduct intense market research, interview customers to find their pain points with products, and build in-depth financial forecasts. The strong real-world experience while in graduate school and the academic rigor of the thesis project allow students to find jobs in a wide variety of industries. Some of the industries include consulting, consumer products, energy, financial services, healthcare, manufacturing, technology, and government.

What was my most impactful project at Notre Dame?
The most impactful project that I worked on while at Notre Dame was in one of my classes, data analytics. The State of Indiana asked the class to mine 7 years of automobile accident history to find patterns in the data and reduce the amount of accidents. I found that introducing rumble strips between two-way, two-land rural roads of 55+ MPH would significantly reduce the amount of vehicular fatalities. My team presented the findings to State representatives, and the State built the proposal into the annual budget for transportation.
What is AES?

Applied Engineering Sciences (AES) is a degree that combines both business and engineering. The engineering college requires calculus 1-3, physics 1-2 for engineers, chemistry, engineering design and engineering modeling. The major requires business classes, such as accounting, statistics, microeconomics, and macroeconomics, and engineering classes, such as statics, circuits, thermodynamics, and materials science. Each student picks a concentration for their degree. The options for concentrations are business law, computer science, media and information, supply chain management, packaging, and technical sales. For more information on the degree, please click here.

What can you do with a degree in AES?
A degree in Applied Engineering Sciences is versatile. It teaches students to be able to bridge the communication gap between technical and non-technical workers. Thus, allowing them to be effective members and managers of interdisciplinary teams. Some recent graduates of the program landed jobs as account executives, product engineers, technical writers, logistics engineers, and operations managers.
What was my coolest project at Michigan State?

The coolest project that I worked on at Michigan State was designing a circuit board tutorial for deaf children in India. It included a fully functional circuit with two switches and two light bulbs. The circuit board was accompanied with YouTube tutorials to explain the system and teach the students how electricity flows through a circuit.
